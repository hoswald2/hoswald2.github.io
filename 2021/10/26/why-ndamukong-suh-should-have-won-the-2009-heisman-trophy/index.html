Why Ndamukong Suh should have won the 2009 Heisman Trophy

2021-10-26

The 2009 college football season was one that had a lot of surprises. Alabama stomped Texas at the Rose Bowl to claim another national championship, the 2009 Fiesta Bowl featured two Group of 5 teams, Boise State and TCU. But one of the biggest surprises from this season was the Heisman Trophy race. Mark Ingram ended up taking home the award, but it left many people confused as they thought it was supposed to go to the best college football player. The Heisman Trophy is an offensive trophy, but that year Ndamukong Suh was clearly college football’s best player and now we will take a look at some data to show how Suh stacked up against the whole national champion Crimson Tide defensive line to show you who the real 2009 Heisman Trophy winner should have been.

Now I can mutate my data to filter out just Ndamukong Suh and the Alabama D-Line.

Suh_tackles <- nebraska_tackles %>% 
  filter(Name == "Ndamukong Suh")
BamaDline_tackles <- bama_tackles %>%
  filter(Pos == "DL")

I wanted to focus on the total tackles that Suh ended up with and compare his total tackle stat, stacked up against the entire Alabama defensive line from the 2009 season.

I took my data from Suh’s stat line and the Alabama stat line and summarized the total number of tackles that were made be each of them. Now I can create a waffle chart with what I found comparing the two dataframes.

Suh_tackles <- c("Total Tackles"=85)
BamaDline_tackles <- c("Total Tackles"=145)

I can even take the total number of tackles from both Suh and the Alabama defensive line and put them in the same data frame.

SuhvBamaDline_tackles <- c("Ndamukong Suh"=85, "Alabame D-Line"=145)

Now I’m ready to create my waffle chart showing my data.

waffle(
        SuhvBamaDline_tackles, 
        rows = 10, 
        title= "2009 Alabama Defensive Line barely out tackles Suh", 
        xlab="1 square = 1 tackle", 
        colors = c("red", "gray")) + labs(caption = "Source: cfbstats.com | By Hayes Oswald", subtitle = "Suh got pretty close.")

As you can see from the waffle chart above, Alabama’s defensive line had more tackles than Ndamukong Suh did. But, we are talking about ONE PLAYER, that totaled more than half of the #1 team, the national champions whole defensive line!

Next I want to focus on how Ndamukong Suh fared against the #1 team in the nation’s defensive line by comparing the two in Tackles For Loss.

Suh_TFL <- nebraska_TFL %>% 
  filter(Name == "Ndamukong Suh")
BamaDline_TFL <- bama_TFL %>%
  filter(Pos == "DL")
TFL <- bind_rows(Suh_TFL, BamaDline_TFL)

Ndamukong Suh should have won the 2009 Heisman Trophy. He almost caught the national champion Alabama’s defensive line in almost every major defensive stat category.
